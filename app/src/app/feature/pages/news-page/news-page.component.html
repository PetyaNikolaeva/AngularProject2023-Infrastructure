The EC approved Bulgaria's European infrastructure program

The European Commission has given the green light to an operational program "Transport connectivity" 2021-2027, the budget of which is 1.61 billion euros. Thus, in the new program period, Bulgaria will be able to finance and implement large-scale infrastructure projects - railway lines, highways, bridges, tunnels, waterways and roads, etc.

Also included are projects for the construction of infrastructure for alternative fuels (charging stations) along the main sections of the country's road network.

The new operational program aims to develop and expand inland waterways and seaports for public transport to carry out multimodal operations. This includes projects for the development and expansion of the port of Lom with the aim of turning it into an intermodal terminal. A project for the expansion of the port of Varna (new quay) is included, which will once again serve to carry out multimodal operations.

The reconstruction of ports is also planned. For example, an anti-flooding facility will be built at the Ruse-Zapad terminal. And more - the delivery of additional multi-functional vessels is planned, through which navigation on the Danube River will be improved. The total estimated budget for these projects is over 403.3 million euros, with the national co-financing amounting to just over 60.4 million euros.

The construction of railway connections to Plovdiv airport and to Burgas airport is also planned, thus aiming to promote intermodality in an urban environment . The budget for these projects is in the amount of just over 47 million euros, with national co-financing in the amount of just over 7 million euros.

The cities that are eligible for participation under the "Green Mobility" project are also indicated. Its aim is to promote sustainable urban mobility through the development of ecological, safe, functional and energy-efficient transport. To improve the state of public urban transport and promote "green mobility", 40 Bulgarian municipalities will be able to apply for funds from the National Plan for Individual objects are marked with pictograms or lines, and different colors indicate the road and railway projects that will be implemented with the respective deadlines in the period until 2040.

Sustainable infrastructure

What are the possibilities and obstacles for its implementation?

More than 60% of the world's greenhouse emissions originate from infrastructure – the transport and energy systems, buildings and industry. At the same time, we feel a growing need for it to be expanded. However, if the new infrastructure is not low-emission and climate-friendly, the Paris Agreement 's targets of increasing the average temperature by less than 2ºC by 2050 will not be met. It must include renewable energy, mass transport and energy efficiency.

About 70% of sustainable infrastructure is needed in developing countries, where its realization is most tangible. The poorest and most socially vulnerable societies are most acutely affected by climate change because they are much more dependent on it. It also has a direct link to development – ​​the construction of renewable energy plants instead of thermal power plants using solid fuel would reduce air pollution and improve the quality of the environment, thereby improving overall health. If we build our cities more compactly, with improved access to public transport, they would also contribute to these higher indicators.
